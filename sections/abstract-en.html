Abstract

The ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy contains OWL classes to classify **buildings**, extending [bot:Building](https://w3id.org/bot#Building). The taxonomy concepts are extracted and restructured from the Onroerend Erfgoed Vlaanderen thesaurus [ERFGOEDTYPES](https://id.erfgoed.net/thesauri/erfgoedtypes) [1,2]. The building types are specialized for classifying heritage buildings. The main taxonomy is structured using RDFS subsumption axioms.

The namespace for ET-BUILDINGS terms is https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/et-buildings#

The suggested prefix for the ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy is et-buildings

The Turtle version of the ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/et-buildings/et-buildings.ttl
The RDF/XML version of the ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/et-buildings/et-buildings.rdf
The JSON-LD version of the ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/et-buildings/et-buildings.jsonld
The N-Triples version of the ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/et-buildings/et-buildings.nt

The ET-BUILDINGS taxonomy was created by Mathias Bonduel (KU Leuven) during his PhD research (2017-2020) funded by the Research Foundation Flanders in the form of a Strategic Basic Research grant (grant agreement 1S65917N).
